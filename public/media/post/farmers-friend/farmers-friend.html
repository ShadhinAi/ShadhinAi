AI-based Disease Diagnosis

Farmers can take pictures of diseased leaves on their smartphones and upload them to the Farmers Friend platform. The AI algorithm analyzes the images and identifies the type of disease and the pest responsible for the infestation. It then provides recommendations on the appropriate pesticide to be used, along with the dosage and application method. The information is also provided in audio form for accessibility.

Solar-powered Irrigation

The project integrates solar panels into irrigation systems that automatically track the sun's movement from east to west, ensuring maximum exposure to sunlight throughout the day. This allows for more efficient utilization of solar energy, reducing the dependency on fossil fuels and lowering operational costs.

Efficient Water Management

The irrigation pump is equipped with sensors that monitor the water level in the field. When the water level reaches the optimal threshold required for crop production, the pump automatically stops, preventing water wastage. As the water level decreases, the pump restarts automatically, ensuring that the crops receive the necessary irrigation without unnecessary water usage.

Audio Instructions

The information provided by the Farmers Friend platform, including disease diagnosis and irrigation instructions, is also available in audio form. This makes it accessible to farmers with limited literacy or language barriers, ensuring that more farmers can benefit from the project.

Accessibility

Crop Disease Management

One of the major challenges faced by farmers is the outbreak of diseases in their crops. Identifying and managing these diseases in a timely manner is critical to prevent crop losses. The Farmers Friend project has come up with an ingenious solution using AI. Farmers can simply take pictures of diseased leaves on their smartphones and upload them to the Farmers Friend platform. The AI algorithm analyzes the images and identifies the type of disease and the pest responsible for the infestation. It then provides recommendations on the appropriate pesticide to be used, along with the dosage and application method. What sets this system apart is that the information is also provided in audio form, making it accessible to farmers with limited literacy or language barriers. This empowers farmers to take prompt action to protect their crops from diseases, leading to higher yields and improved livelihoods.

Solar-Powered Irrigation:

Access to reliable irrigation is crucial for crop production, especially in areas where water is scarce. The Farmers Friend project has taken a sustainable approach by integrating solar panels into irrigation systems. These solar panels are designed to automatically track the sun's movement from east to west, ensuring maximum exposure to sunlight throughout the day. This enables the system to harness more solar energy, reducing the dependence on fossil fuels and lowering operational costs for farmers. Solar-powered irrigation also mitigates greenhouse gas emissions, contributing to a greener and more sustainable agriculture sector.

Efficient Water Management

Water wastage is a significant concern in agriculture, as excessive use of water can deplete precious water resources and increase costs for farmers. The Farmers Friend project has developed an automatic system to address this issue. The irrigation pump is equipped with sensors that monitor the water level in the field. When the water level reaches the optimal threshold required for crop production, the pump automatically stops, preventing water wastage. As the water level decreases, the pump restarts automatically, ensuring that the crops receive the necessary irrigation without unnecessary water usage. This intelligent water management system helps farmers optimize their water resources, conserve water, and reduce irrigation costs.

Additionally, the audio instructions provided by the Farmers Friend platform make the information accessible to farmers with limited literacy or language barriers, ensuring that more farmers can benefit from the project.

Summary of Bangladeshi robot Mina

Three students of the Electronics Department of Lakshipur Polytechnic Institute, Shantanu Acharjee, Mehraj Hossain Sagar, and Yasin Arafat Riday, have made a robot named Meena to entertain children as well as to study. As a result, the robot will spend some time away from the child's mobile phone and spend time with Meena. The data of what we are saying through the microphone is going to the Raspberry Pi computer in Meena. And this entire process is done using Python programming language. She has a range of facial expressions and can mimic human emotions, making her seem more empathetic and relatable. Shantonu Acharjee's work on Meena is a testament to the potential of technology to make a positive impact on the world, and it will be exciting to see how robots like Meena continue to evolve in the future.
